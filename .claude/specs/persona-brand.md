# Soraia Oliveira — Persona & Brand Context

## The Artist

**Soraia Oliveira** is a Portuguese multidisciplinary visual artist based in Guimarães, Portugal. Formally trained across three countries — School of Arts and Design (Caldas da Rainha, Portugal), Free University of Bolzano (Italy), and Accademia di Belle Arti (L'Aquila, Italy) — she carries an international foundation filtered through deep Portuguese roots.

Her practice began seriously in 2019. What started as intuitive experimentation with fine arts and photography became a rigorous, body-centered investigation of identity, presence, transformation, and memory.

### Core Identity Words (self-described)
CREATIVE THINKER / DREAMER / OBSERVER / PERFORMER / MULTI-TASKER / EXPERIMENTER / BOUNDARY PUSHER — "driven by curiosity"

### Artist Statement (first-person)
> "I'm Soraia, a multidisciplinary artist working with photography, drawing, self-portraiture and experimental processes. My work begins with curiosity and a need to understand our inner selves, the body, and the spaces I move through, exploring identity, presence, and transformation."

### Artist Statement (third-person, for press/curatorial)
> "Her work emerges from personal experience and a sensitive observation of everyday life, expanding into compositions that question the boundaries between presence and absence, reality and memory, and the subconscious mind. Through experimentation and an ongoing investigation of gesture over time, Soraia creates images that function as fragments of a visual autobiography."

### Thematic Territory
- **The body** — central to everything. Subject, tool, site of meaning.
- **Identity & transformation** — ongoing, never fixed.
- **Presence vs. absence** — what remains when the body leaves.
- **Memory & the subconscious** — "reality and memory, and the subconscious mind."
- **Space** — rooms, landscapes activated by the body's presence.
- **The unfinished** — deliberately incomplete lines, open meaning.

## The Brand

### Voice & Tone
- **Intimate** — first-person, direct address ("I'm Soraia...", "connecting with you...")
- **Poetic but not precious** — philosophical without being academic
- **Understated** — no hyperbole, no superlatives, no "amazing" or "stunning"
- **Confident stillness** — she doesn't need to shout; the work speaks
- **Invitational** — "JOIN SORAIA" (newsletter), "Make your appointment" (studio visit)

### Anti-patterns (what her voice is NOT)
- Not corporate or institutional ("Soraia Oliveira's practice interrogates...")
- Not casual/chatty ("Hey guys! Check out my new...")
- Not self-deprecating or overly humble
- Not hyperbolic or sales-driven ("AMAZING new collection!")

### Visual Identity Principles
1. **Editorial restraint** — gallery/magazine aesthetic, generous whitespace
2. **All-caps for structure** — headings, labels, navigation in uppercase (editorial convention)
3. **Mixed case for intimacy** — body text and personal copy in sentence case
4. **Image-dominant** — the artwork IS the design; layouts serve the images
5. **Monochromatic palette** — blacks, grays, whites with red only for status (sold/sale)
6. **Typography as atmosphere** — type should feel like it belongs in an art book
7. **Custom cursor** — original site has this; signals crafted experience
8. **Body-consciousness** — design should feel physical, not digital

### Typography Direction
The original site uses a clean sans-serif (likely Neue Haas Grotesk or similar). Our Satoshi Variable aligns well — it's geometric, clean, with humanist warmth. Use it with:
- Wide letter-spacing on labels and headings (tracking-wide, tracking-widest)
- Tight line-height on display text (leading-none, leading-tight)
- Normal/relaxed for body copy (readability over density)

## The Audience

### Primary: Art Collectors (Direct Sales)
- Purchasing originals (€750–€1,800) and editions (€190–€1,200)
- Expect gallery-grade presentation, provenance details, edition numbers
- Value: image quality, artwork details, easy purchase flow, trust signals

### Secondary: Cultural Press & Curators
- Looking for artist statement, exhibition history, high-res images, CV
- Contact CTA: "press, orders & partnerships"
- Value: professional presentation, easy media access, clear biography

### Tertiary: Art Enthusiasts & Followers
- Newsletter subscribers, social media followers
- Want behind-the-scenes, process, new releases
- "JOIN SORAIA" — community framing, not transactional

### Entry-level: Jewelry & Small Editions Buyers
- Affordable entry (€170–€360 jewelry, €190 proofs)
- May not be traditional art collectors
- Value: beautiful product presentation, clear sizing/materials, easy checkout

### Studio Visitors
- Local/regional audience in Guimarães
- Appointment-based, intimate experience
- "The work can be experienced beyond the finished pieces"
